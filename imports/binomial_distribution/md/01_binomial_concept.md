# What is the Binomial Distribution?

## Explanation

The binomial distribution models the number of successes observed in a fixed number of independent trials when each trial has two possible outcomes and the probability of success remains constant.

## Conditions

- There is a fixed number of trials.

- Each trial is independent of the others.

- Each trial has two possible outcomes, usually described as success or failure.

- The probability of success is the same on every trial.

## Why it Matters

The binomial distribution is widely used in public health and statistics to model outcomes such as positive test results, treatment success, and vaccination uptake across a defined group.

## Tabs

Definition :: The binomial distribution models the number of successes across repeated independent trials.

Example :: A coin is tossed 10 times and the number of heads is counted.

Interpretation :: The model helps us estimate how likely different numbers of successes are within a fixed number of trials.
